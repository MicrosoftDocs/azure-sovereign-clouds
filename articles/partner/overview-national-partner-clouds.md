# National Partner Clouds

National Partner Clouds are a core component of the Microsoft Sovereign Cloud strategy. They provide independently operated cloud environments that deliver Microsoft Azure and Microsoft 365 capabilities under local ownership and control. This cloud delivery model lets governments and critical infrastructure providers meet stringent sovereignty, compliance, and security requirements.

These offerings are designed for scenarios where full ownership and operational independence from Microsoft is required, such as national security, public sector regulation, or geopolitical risk mitigation.

## Why National Partner Clouds

In an era of increasing digital regulation and geopolitical complexity, many governments and regulated industries require:

- Local control over infrastructure and operations
- Compliance with national laws and certification frameworks
- Resilience against foreign jurisdictional influence
- Operational transparency and auditability

## Key characteristics

### Local ownership and operation

Local entities operate National Partner Clouds under national law. Microsoft provides the technology stack, but the cloud is:

- Physically and logically isolated
- Managed by local personnel
- Governed by national compliance frameworks

Examples include:

- Bleu (France): A joint venture between Orange and Capgemini, designed to meet SecNumCloud requirements.
- Delos Cloud (Germany): Operated by an SAP subsidiary, aligned with German Cloud Platform Requirements.

### Integrated Microsoft capabilities

Although they're independently operated, National Partner Clouds deliver:

- Microsoft Azure: Compute, storage, networking, and identity services
- Microsoft 365: Productivity tools including Exchange, SharePoint, and Teams

## Implementation scenarios

National Partner Clouds are ideal for:

- Countries/regions with strict, specific sovereignty laws and regulations
- Public sector and defense workloads
- Critical infrastructure providers
